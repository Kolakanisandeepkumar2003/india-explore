Favourite Places

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a beautiful white marble monument located in Agra. It was built by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. The construction of the Taj Mahal was completed in 1653. It is considered a symbol of love and one of the Seven Wonders of the World. The monument is famous for its stunning architecture and symmetry. It is situated on the banks of the Yamuna River. Millions of tourists visit the Taj Mahal every year.

Golden Temple

The Golden Temple, also known as Harmandir Sahib, is located in Amritsar. It is the holiest shrine of Sikhism and a major pilgrimage site. The temple was founded by Guru Ram Das in the 16th century. It is famous for its beautiful golden structure and sacred pool called the Amrit Sarovar. The temple offers free meals (langar) to thousands of people every day. People from all religions are welcome to visit the Golden Temple. It is a symbol of equality, peace, and spiritual devotion.

Mysore Palace

The Mysore Palace is a famous historical palace in Mysuru. It is also known as Amba Vilas Palace. The palace was the royal residence of the Wodeyar Dynasty. It is built in a beautiful Indo-Saracenic style of architecture. The interiors are richly decorated with paintings, carvings, and stained glass. The palace looks stunning when illuminated with thousands of lights at night. It is especially popular during the grand Mysore Dasara celebrations.

Varanasi Temple

The Kashi Vishwanath Temple in Varanasi is one of the most sacred Hindu temples. It is dedicated to Lord Shiva, one of the principal deities in Hinduism. The temple is situated on the banks of the holy Ganga River. It attracts millions of pilgrims and tourists every year. The temple is known for its golden dome and spiritual atmosphere. Varanasi is one of the oldest living cities in the world. Devotees believe that visiting the temple helps attain salvation (moksha).

The Taj Mahal is considered to be the greatest architectural achievement in the whole range of Indo-Islamic architecture. Its recognised architectonic beauty has a rhythmic combination of solids and voids, concave and convex and light shadow; such as arches and domes further increases the aesthetic aspect. Not a piece of architecture, as other buildings are, but the proud passions of an emperor’s love wrought in living stones.

Golden Temple

The temple was founded by Guru Ram Das in the 16th century. The temple is built in the middle of a sacred water tank called Amrit Sarovar. It is one of the most important cultural and religious landmarks in India.
